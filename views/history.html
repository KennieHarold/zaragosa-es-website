Our History

“Every humble institution has its own story to tell. Its history will speak for its very existence.”

The school was established in 1930 , before the World War II. There were only Grade I and 2. It was located in the house of Amilo Almojera. Due to the increased of enrolment, additional 2 classes were added. The school was subsequently moved to its present location . In 1943 the school was closed due to the war. The Japanese used the school as a garrison or military post. Prisoners and suspected enemies where incarcerated. They were tortured and brutally killed. It was believed that many were buried there. When the war was over, the school was opened with six(6) classes, making it a complete elementary school. Buildings were erected to replace the temporary school rooms. Through the years improvement were done. Enrolment increased and additional teachers were assigned.

Zaragosa Elementary School is a consistent winner be it academic or non academic in district , division and up to the national level. Investigatory Projects in Science won in the Division and Regional Level. The school has produced a national winner in the Dr. Juan Salcedo award in Science in 1994 and 1995 in the person of Mrs. Anita Almojera. Mrs. Merlene C. Gaona was also awarded as an Outstanding Teacher in the Municipaity in 2016 and 2017 in the Province of La Union. Remarkable achievements in the past that the good name of the school was maintained. Mrs Edna B. Agustin and Mrs. Delia Canave past principals of Zaragosa Elementary School was promoted to District Supervisor. At present the school is led by the visionary and Outstanding District Principal 2019 in the person of Miss Yazmin Rose B. Agustin. The teachers and the school heads ,past and present worked real hard together with its stakeholders to achieve the schools vision and mission.
